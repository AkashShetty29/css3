A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Missile Man of India

APJ Abdul Kalam, full name Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, was an Indian scientist, statesman, and 11th President of India. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India, and passed away on July 27, 2015, in Shillong, Meghalaya, India. Kalam played a pivotal role in India's space and missile development programs. He served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and as the Secretary of the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) from 1992 to 1999. Kalam was also known as the "Missile Man of India" for his significant contributions to India's ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology. During his career, Kalam played a crucial role in the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle, the SLV-III, and the operationalization of strategic missiles such as Agni and Prithvi. He was also involved in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998. Kalam's contributions to the field of science and technology were recognized internationally. He received several prestigious awards, including the Padma Bhushan (1981), Padma Vibhushan (1990), and the Bharat Ratna (1997), which is India's highest civilian honor. Kalam was also a prolific writer and penned various books, including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds," which became popular among students and the general public. In 2002, Kalam was elected as the 11th President of India and served in this capacity until 2007. During his presidency, he focused on youth development, education, and societal transformation. He often spoke to students and inspired them to dream big and work towards realizing their goals. APJ Abdul Kalam's life and work continue to inspire people around the world, particularly in the fields of science, education, and youth empowerment. He is remembered as a visionary leader and a true role model for generations to come.

  4. In 2002, he became the 11th President of India and the first scientist to hold this position. During his presidency, he focused on education, youth development, and rural development.
  5. Kalam authored several books, including his autobiography "Wings of Fire," and continued to inspire people through his speeches and interactions with students. He passed away on July 27, 2015, leaving behind a lasting legacy.
